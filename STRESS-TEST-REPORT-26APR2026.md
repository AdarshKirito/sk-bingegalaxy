## Executive Summary

The booking, payment, JWT, and `/api/v1/{svc}/admin/*` authorization paths are in good shape — input validation is thorough, signatures are verified (alg:none rejected), client-supplied identity headers are stripped at the gateway, ownership is enforced on booking reads, and idempotency-key dedupe works end-to-end. However the test surfaced **one production-grade CRITICAL: any logged-in CUSTOMER can read and write the entire loyalty `super-admin` configuration** — including renaming the program and adding tiers (verified by mutation; restored). The gateway’s `isAdminPath` matcher only covers `/api/v1/{service}/admin/*` and the v2 super-admin controller assumed the gateway would do the work, so neither layer enforces the role and the documented contract is silently broken. Secondary risks worth fixing soon: bookings accept dates years in the future with no upper bound; emoji/RTL notes are rejected as malformed bodies hurting real mobile users; cross-customer cancel/reschedule return 400 instead of 403 leaking resource existence; and the only thing preventing exact-duplicate booking POSTs (when no idempotency key is sent) is the per-user rate limiter — there is no content-based dedupe in the booking service. Patch the loyalty authz gap immediately; the rest are MEDIUM/LOW.
